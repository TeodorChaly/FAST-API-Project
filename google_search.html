In the EP, Staķis wants to work with people who previously worked in his office

LETA
June 25, 2024
Facebook
Twitter

    Mārtiņš Staķis, who was elected to the European Parliament from the Latvian political party “Progressives”, has said he wants to work with people who previously worked in his office during his term as Mayor of Riga.

    In his interview to LTV programme Rīta panorāma, Staķis said he looks towards his old office.

    At the same time, he said he has only started working on putting together his team.

    “I know multiple of my colleagues, such as Reinis Pozņaks, have matured for the Committee on Security and Defence, where I will definitely want to work. I hope multiple Latvian MEPs will meet there, because this way we will be able to coordinate our activities and fight together for Latvia’s interests,” said Staķis.

    LETA previously reported that in Latvia the European Parliament elections were won by New Unity and the National Alliance – both political parties will receive two seats in the European Parliament. Other parties that secured seats in the European Parliament include For Latvia’s Development, Combined List, Progressives, Harmony, and Latvia in First Place: these parties will have one seat each.
